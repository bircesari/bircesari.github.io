How to Build Effective BI Dashboards

November 14, 2025 5 min read Business Intelligence, Dashboards

In my years of working with business intelligence, I've seen countless dashboards, some that transform decision-making and others that collect digital dust. Here's what I've learned about building dashboards that actually get used.

1. Start with the Questions, Not the Data

The biggest mistake in dashboard design? Starting with "what data do we have?" instead of "what decisions need to be made?" Before touching any tool, I always conduct stakeholder interviews to understand:

  • What decisions are you trying to make?
  • What questions keep you up at night?
  • What would success look like in 6 months?

2. The 5-Second Rule

If your dashboard can't communicate its main insight in 5 seconds, it's too complex. I use the "executive elevator test"â€”could your CEO understand the key message while riding an elevator?

"A dashboard should answer questions, not raise them." - My mentor's best advice

3. Visual Hierarchy Matters

Your eyes should naturally flow to the most important information first. Here's my hierarchy framework:

  1. Top-left: Most critical KPI
  2. Top-right: Comparison or trend
  3. Middle: Supporting details
  4. Bottom: Drill-down capabilities

Real-World Example

At Zorlu Holding, we had a dashboard with 47 metrics. Usage was low, and executives complained about information overload. We redesigned it to show just 5 key metrics with the ability to drill down. Result? Daily active users increased by 300%.

Key Takeaways

  • Less is more: focus on decision-driving metrics
  • Test with actual users before full rollout
  • Update based on feedback, not assumptions
  • Mobile-first design is no longer optional
